1  What this tool is (in one minute)

Taxscan Push sends short notifications straight to your readers' web browsers — even when they aren't on the Taxscan website — to tell them about important new updates. A reader "subscribes" once by clicking Allow on a prompt; after that, the notifications appear on their device.

The platform does most of the work on its own:

  • It watches Taxscan's articles as they're published.
  • It automatically sends the important ones (e.g. Supreme Court & High Court rulings, ICAI, CBDT, CBIC, DGFT, IBBI, GSTAT, PMLA updates).
  • It holds back the less important ones and spaces sends out so readers aren't overwhelmed.
  • It asks you to decide on the few articles it isn't sure about (analysis/explainer pieces).

So your role is simple: review and supervise, and send things manually when you want to. This guide shows you how.

2  Logging in & your account

1. Go to https://push.taxscan.in/admin/ in any browser.
2. Enter your email and password, then click Log in.
3. If this is your first time, you'll be given a temporary password and asked to set your own. Pick something strong and private.

Two kinds of account

RoleWhat they can do
AdministratorEverything below, plus add/remove team members and change roles (the Users screen).
PublisherUse the Dashboard, Review queue, Compose/send, Campaigns and Activity. Cannot manage other users.
Tip — changing your password You can change your password any time from the Change password link in the top menu.
Keep your login private Every action you take (sending, approving, rejecting) is recorded against your name in the Activity log. Don't share your account.

3  The big picture: how notifications go out

Understanding this makes everything else obvious. There are three things the system does with every new article:

① It decides whether the article is worth a notification

CategoryWhat happens
SEND  Major authorities
Supreme Court, High Court, ICAI, CBDT, CBIC, DGFT, IBBI, GSTAT, PMLA		 Queued to be sent automatically.
HOLD  Tribunal orders
ITAT, CESTAT, NCLAT, NCLT		 Not sent (these were never pushed historically). Used only as filler if there's genuinely nothing else to send.
REVIEW  Everything else
Analysis & explainer articles		 Sent to the Review queue for you to approve, push, or reject.

② It paces the sending so readers aren't spammed

  • At most one push every ~45 minutes.
  • Up to ~20 notifications per day in total.
  • Quiet hours overnight (roughly 11 pm–7 am) — nothing sends while people sleep.
  • When several articles are waiting, it sends the most important one first (Supreme Court > High Court > regulatory), and the others wait their turn — they are never lost.

③ It lets you override, instantly, whenever you want

Automatic pacing is for the routine flow. For anything urgent, you are always in control — use Push now in the Review queue, or Force send in Compose (both explained later). These go out immediately to everyone.

Why an article didn't go out the second it was published This is normal and intended — the system spaces sends ~45 minutes apart to protect readers. The article isn't lost; it's waiting its turn. If it's urgent, send it yourself with Push now / Force send.

4  Your daily routine — the 5-minute check

You don't need to babysit the system. A short daily glance is enough:

1. Open the Dashboard. Look at the coloured dots next to each number. green = healthy. If everything's green, you're done with this part.
2. Open the Review queue. Decide on anything waiting there — Approve the good analysis pieces, Push now the urgent ones, Reject the rest. Aim to leave it empty.
3. (Optional) Send anything special. Big breaking story? Use Compose to send it yourself.
That's the whole job Glance at the Dashboard, clear the Review queue. Everything else runs automatically.

5  Reading the Dashboard

The Dashboard is your health monitor. Each metric card has a small (i) icon — hover it for a plain explanation. Cards that have a target show a coloured dot:

Green — on target    Amber — warning, keep an eye on it    Red — below target, needs attention

What every number means & what to expect

CardGood looks likeWhat it means & what to do
Active subscribersSteadily rising How many people are signed up to receive notifications right now — your live audience. Expect steady growth. A small sudden drop is usually just dead subscriptions being cleaned out, not lost readers.
Total sentAlways rising The running total of notifications delivered since launch. It only ever goes up. A faster climb means more is going out and/or your audience is bigger.
Overall CTR
click-through rate		≥ 6%   4–6% Of the notifications sent, the share that people actually clicked. The best gauge of how interesting and relevant your pushes are. If it's low, the headlines or the choice of what to send need work.
Opt-in rate≥ 5%   3–5% Of the people shown the "Allow notifications?" prompt, the share who said yes. Measures how convincing the prompt is. Low usually means it appears at an awkward moment.
Delivery rate≥ 95%   90–95% Of the notifications we tried to send, the share that actually reached the device. The rest bounce off dead/expired subscriptions. A dip usually self-heals as dead ones are removed.
Unsubscribe rate< 0.5% The share of subscribers who opted out or whose subscription died. Lower is better. A spike is a fatigue warning — usually too many notifications or content people didn't want.

The lower sections

  • Subscriber sources — where your audience came from. Recaptured (returning visitors who already allowed notifications) is normally the biggest and should keep climbing. Soft prompt = fresh opt-ins from the on-site prompt.
  • 30-day subscriber growth — a simple line of new sign-ups per day. Rising or steady = healthy; flat near zero = sign-ups have stalled.
  • Opt-in funnel — the journey from "prompt shown" → "accepted" → "subscribed". A big drop between two steps shows where readers are being lost.
  • Recent campaigns — your latest notifications and how each performed (who it reached, clicks, CTR). Great for spotting which headlines land best.
Don't over-react to one number Rates need a reasonable volume to be meaningful. One slow day, or a tiny send, can swing a percentage. Look at the trend over several days, not a single reading.

6  The Review queue — your main task

Open the Review tab daily. It lists the articles the system wasn't sure about — mostly analysis and explainer pieces that don't name a specific court, plus job/recruitment posts (see below). For each one you have three choices:

ButtonWhat it doesUse it when…
Approve Adds it to the normal send queue. It goes out on the next available slot, alongside everything else. It's a good piece worth sending, but not urgent.
Push now Sends it immediately to everyone, skipping the wait. It's important and you want it out right away.
Reject Drops it — it will never be sent. It's not worth a notification.
Goal: leave the queue empty Clearing it daily keeps the channel fresh and means no good article sits unsent. There's no penalty for rejecting — when in doubt, reject; you're curating quality.
Good to know Tribunal orders (ITAT, CESTAT, NCLAT, NCLT) do not appear here — they're held automatically and need no action from you.

Job postings

Job and recruitment posts from the Job Scan section (CA / CMA / MBA vacancies, hiring, walk-ins, internships) now land in this Review tab automatically. The system never sends a job post on its own — it's always your call. Use the same three buttons: Push now sends it to everyone straight away, Approve schedules it for the next slot, Reject drops it.

Handling job posts
  • Job posts go to all subscribers (there's no separate "jobs" audience), so push only the ones genuinely worth interrupting everyone for.
  • Even a post that names a body (e.g. “ICAI Recruitment”) comes here rather than going out automatically — that's intentional.
  • First time only: you'll see a one-time batch of current vacancies, because the feed was just switched on. Work through them once (reject the ones not worth a push); after that only new postings appear.

7  Sending a notification yourself (Compose)

Use the Compose screen to write and send a notification by hand — for a big story, an announcement, or anything you want out on your own terms.

The fields

FieldWhat to put
TitleThe headline the reader sees. Keep it short and punchy — this is what makes them click.
BodyOne line of detail under the title.
Click URLThe page that opens when they tap the notification — usually a taxscan.in article, but you can also link a course (academy.taxscan.in) or a product (shop.taxscan.in).
Icon URL (optional)Leave blank to use the Taxscan logo.
TargetAll subscribers, or By topic (GST, Income Tax, Customs, Corporate) to reach only people interested in that area.
Choosing who receives it — check this every time
  • Compose opens on All subscribers. Leave it there for anything meant for the whole base (results, circulars, major rulings) — that's the normal choice.
  • Switch to By topic only when you deliberately want a slice. Most subscribers picked specific topics, so a single-topic push reaches only that slice (plus the few "All news" readers) — it can land on a very small number of people.
  • If the reach shown after sending looks far below your usual full base (~all subscribers), the target was probably narrowed by accident — just re-send with All subscribers selected. A duplicate only means a few people get it twice, which is harmless.
  • To preview without sending to anyone, use Test on this device (below) — it goes only to your own browser, never to subscribers.

The two important switches

SwitchWhat it does & when to use it
Breaking Lets the notification go out even during quiet hours (overnight). Use only for genuinely urgent news. It does not change who receives it.
Force send
(full reach)		 Reaches every eligible subscriber right now, ignoring the normal spacing and frequency limits. Use for a major story you want everyone to get immediately. (Without this, a hand-send still respects the usual pacing.)

Scheduling

  • Send now or Schedule for later (pick a date & time). Note: Force send is immediate only.

Test on this device (preview before you send)

Below the compose box is a Test on this device panel — the easy way to see exactly how a notification will look before sending it to anyone.

  • Click Enable test notifications on this device once, and accept the browser's permission prompt.
  • Then click Send test to my device — the notification you composed appears on your browser only. No one else receives it.
  • If notifications are blocked, allow them for this site in your browser settings and reload the page.
Always preview the important ones Send a test to your own device first for anything that matters — a course or product link, a webinar, or any major push — so you can check the title, wording and that the link opens the right page.
Before you hit send Double-check the Title (no typos — readers see it instantly) and the Click URL (open it once to be sure it's the right page). A notification can't be "unsent".
Good push, in 3 rules Short, specific title · link straight to the article · send only what's genuinely worth interrupting someone for.

The Reports screen — coverage at a glance

The Reports tab shows two heat-maps for a period — categories × dates and courts/benches × dates — with the count in each cell shaded red (none) to green (plenty), plus a summary line (total articles, change vs the previous period, and where coverage was missing). It answers "did we publish enough, across enough of the map?" Switch between Weekly and Monthly at the top.

  • Share it — Download image or Copy image turns the whole report into a picture you can drop straight into a WhatsApp group.
  • It also arrives by email automatically — every Monday morning (the past week) and on the 1st of each month (the previous month).
  • Email me a test sends the current report to your own inbox so you can see how it looks.
Who gets the report email (Admins) Everyone with a login receives it. To include other internal colleagues who don't need a login, add their email under Email recipients on the Reports screen — they'll get the weekly & monthly email only, nothing else. This report is internal: it never goes to your notification subscribers.
Give it a week The courts/benches heat-map is accurate right away. The categories heat-map fills in over the first week as new articles are tagged — early on it may show mostly "Uncategorised."

10  Frequently asked questions

Why didn't my article go out immediately?

To protect readers, the system sends about one notification every 45 minutes. Your article is in the queue and will go out on its turn — it isn't lost. For urgent items, use Push now or Force send.

Why aren't ITAT / CESTAT / NCLAT / NCLT stories being sent?

By long-standing editorial policy these tribunal orders aren't pushed — they'd dilute the channel. The system applies this automatically. If you ever want to send one, you can do it by hand in Compose.

How do I send something to everyone, right now?

In Compose, make sure Target is set to All subscribers (the default), write it and tick Force send. Or, for an item already in the Review queue, click Push now.

A reader says they didn't receive a notification.

Common reasons: they never subscribed (or unsubscribed), their browser blocked notifications at the OS level, or their subscription expired (counted in the delivery rate). This is normal — no system reaches 100%.

Can I undo a send?

No — once a notification is out, it's out. Always double-check the title and link before sending.

What's the difference between "Breaking" and "Force send"?

Breaking only lets a send happen during overnight quiet hours. Force send makes it reach everyone immediately, ignoring the normal spacing/frequency limits. They're independent — you can use either or both.

How often should I check in?

Once a day is plenty: a quick Dashboard glance and clearing the Review queue. The system handles the rest.

Need help? For anything this guide doesn't cover — a number that looks wrong, a screen behaving oddly, or a question about strategy — reach out to your administrator.
